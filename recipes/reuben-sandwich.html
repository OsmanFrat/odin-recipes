Reuben Sandwich

What Is a Reuben Sandwich?

A Reuben is a grilled sandwich featuring corned beef on rye bread. The Reuben is a menu
staple in Jewish-style delicatessens, but it's not technically kosher because it combines meat and cheese.

Ingredients

  • 8 slices rye bread
  • Â½ cup Thousand Island dressing
  • 8 slices Swiss cheese
  • 8 slices deli slices corned beef
  • 1 cup sauerkraut, drained
  • 2 tablespoons butter, softened

Directions

  1. Preheat a large griddle or skillet over medium heat.
  2. Spread one side of bread slices evenly with Thousand Island dressing. On four bread slices,
    layer one slice Swiss cheese, 2 slices corned beef, 1/4 cup sauerkraut, and a second slice of
    Swiss cheese. Top with remaining bread slices, dressing-side down. Butter the top of each sandwich.
  3. Place sandwiches, butter-side down on the preheated griddle; butter the top of each
    sandwich with remaining butter. Grill until both sides are golden brown, about 5 minutes per side.Serve hot.
